Introduction

Constitutional rights are the fundamental human rights of citizens that are protected by a nation's constitution. These rights form the cornerstone of democratic societies and ensure individual freedoms while promoting social justice and equality.[1]

Fundamental Rights

Civil Liberties

  • Freedom of speech
  • Freedom of religion
  • Right to privacy
  • Due process of law
  • Protection against discrimination

Political Rights

  • Right to vote
  • Right to assembly
  • Right to petition
  • Freedom of association
  • Political participation

Social Rights

  • Right to education
  • Right to healthcare
  • Labor rights
  • Social security
  • Environmental rights

Legal Framework

Constitutional rights are protected through a complex legal framework that includes constitutional provisions, statutory laws, and judicial interpretations. This framework ensures that rights are not only declared but also effectively enforced.[2]
